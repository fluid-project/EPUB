Chapter 3 - Adding Forces

Forces can be added and subtracted to give a resulting net force. To understand the basics of calculating net force, let us consider a game of tug-of-war.

Players, who we call "Pullers," form two teams on opposite ends of a rope and pull against each other until one team is pulled over the centre line. When the match starts, each team exerts force in opposite directions, and the team with the greatest combined force will win because the net force is going in their direction.

Example:

Blue team chooses two pullers capable pulling 50 N and 100 N to the left. Red team selects three pullers who can pull 50 N, 50 N, and 100 N to the right.

To figure out which team will win, calculate the net force by adding up the forces of each team.

For Blue team, the total force is 50 N + 100 N = 150 N.

For Red team, the total force is 50 N + 50 N + 100 N = 200 N.

( 50 + 100 ) - ( 50 + 50 + 100 )
= ( 150 ) - ( 200 )
= -50 N

Net force is 150 N minus 200 N (we are subtracting 200 N because Red team is pulling in the opposite direction of Blue team). Therefore the total force is -50 N, which means Red team wins.

Tug-of-War Interactive Game

The following is an interactive simulation of a tug-of-war game with the Blue team on the left side and the Red team on the right. Each team can consist of 4 pullers, and each member pulls with different force. The team with the greatest combined force will win the round.

Instructions

  1. Assemble the Blue team by choosing from any of the four available pullers: large, medium, or one of two small pullers.
    • Place pullers by dragging and dropping the pullers onto the rope.
    • If using a keyboard or a switch device, move focus (by selecting Tab key if using a keyboard) until a Blue focus outline appears around a puller and activate (by selecting Space or Enter keys on a keyboard) to place them.
  2. Next, assemble the Red team by the same way you assembled the Blue team. If using a switch device or keyboard, you may need to move focus until the a Red puller is focused.
  3. Once both teams are assembled, select the "Go" button to start the match! If using a keyboard or switch device, you may need to move focus a few times until focus appears on the "Go" button.
  4. Watch the two teams pull their way to a victory or to a draw.
  5. Select the "Return" button to reset the match.
