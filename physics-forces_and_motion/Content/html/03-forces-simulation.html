Chapter 3 - Adding Forces

Forces can be added and subtracted to give a resulting net force. To understand the basics of calculating net force, let us consider a game of tug-of-war.

Players from two teams form groups on opposite ends of a rope and pull against each other until one team wins by pulling the other team over the centre line. When the match starts, each team exerts force in opposite directions, and the team with the greatest combined force will win because the net force is going in their direction.

Example:

Left team chooses two members capable pulling 50 N and 100 N to the left. Right team selects three members who can pull 50 N, 50 N, and 100 N to the right.

To figure out which team will win, calculate the net force by adding up the forces of each team.

For the left team, the total force is 50 N + 100 N = 150 N.

For the right team, the total force is 50 N + 50 N + 100 N = 200 N.

( 50 + 100 ) - ( 50 + 50 + 100 )
= ( 150 ) - ( 200 )
= -50 N

Net force is 150 N minus 200 N (we are subtracting 200 N because right team is pulling in the opposite direction of left team). Therefore the total force is -50 N, which means right team wins.

Forces and Motion Interactive Games

The following is a collection of interactive games exploring the concepts of forces and motion. There are 4 games to choose from: Tug-of-War, Motion, Friction, and Acceleration. Feel free to choose and experiment with these games.

The following is a detailed description of the tug-of-war game.

A team is on the left (the blue team) and another team (the red team) is on the right. Each team can consist of 4 pullers, and each member pulls with different force. The team with the greatest combined force will win the round.

Instructions

  1. Assemble the left team by choosing from any of the four available team members: large, medium, or one of two small team members. Place people onto the rope by dragging and dropping the pullers onto the rope.
  2. Next, assemble the right team by the same way you assembled the left team.
  3. Once both teams are assembled, select the "Go" button to start the match! If using a keyboard or switch device, you may need to move focus a few times until focus appears on the "Go" button.
  4. Watch the two teams pull their way to a victory or to a draw.
  5. Select the "Return" button to reset the match.
